Making traditional South Indian filter coffee is an art that has been passed down through generations. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you brew the perfect cup of filter coffee.

Ingredients

  • Freshly ground coffee powder (preferably South Indian blend)
  • Water
  • Milk
  • Sugar (optional)

Equipment

  • South Indian coffee filter
  • Stove or heating element
  • Serving cups
  • Spoon

Steps to Make Filter Coffee

  1. Boil water in a kettle or pot.
  2. Add 2-3 tablespoons of coffee powder to the upper compartment of the coffee filter.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the coffee powder in the filter.
  4. Place the lid on the filter and let the coffee brew for 10- 15 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, heat milk in a separate pot until it is hot but not boiling.
  6. Once the coffee has brewed, pour the decoction into a cup.
  7. Add hot milk to the decoction in a 1:1 ratio (adjust to taste).
  8. Add sugar if desired and stir well.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your traditional South Indian filter coffee!

Enjoy your cup of traditional South Indian filter coffee, a perfect blend of strong coffee and creamy milk!
